The Problem:

Georgia:

  • Has some of the highest maternal mortality rates in the nation (37.9 deaths per 100,000 live births with 87% deemed preventable)
  • 34.6 of counties defined as maternal health deserts
  • Women in Maternity care deserts may need to travel three time further than those in areas with full access

Healthcare Access Challenge in Hancock County

Hancock County, Georgia:
  • Located in the rural eastern part of Georgia and classified as maternal health desert
  • Mothers must travel approx. 63 miles (over an hour) to access prenatal or maternal care

Barriers such as lack of transportation, inability to take time off work, childcare responsibilities, and other socioeconomic challenges often cause women to miss or delay vital appointments. Our program seeks to remove these barriers by bringing care directly to the community.

By providing accessible, preventive, and community-based maternal care, our program aims to:

  • Reduce maternal mortality and complications associated with pregnancy
  • Strengthen the relationship between healthcare providers and the community
  • Promote healthier outcomes for mothers and babies in Hancock County

Mother's Aid 2-Year Pilot Program Timeline

Month 1

Community Recruitment

Community Health Workers (CHWs) go into the community and recruit 20 first trimester mothers.

Month 1 (cont.)

Initial Appointment Coordination

CHWs will coordinate a first appointment at participating Piedmont hospital to establish care with a physician. If they are already a patient, we will make sure that they have this initial pregnancy appointment.

Months 2-11

Monthly Home Visits

Monthly home visits to participating mothers (~5 patients a week). Give ultrasound, bloodwork, and supplements and then send off results to their doctor for their Telehealth visit.

Months 12-24

Postpartum Health Services

Begin postpartum health services on a scheduled by patient basis. We will provide physicals, blood work, glucose monitoring, and optional Telehealth services. We will send these results off to their doctors for Telehealth visits.

Program Overview

This project is designed as a 2-year pilot program. Overall design of the program is to enroll 20 pregnant women who are in their first trimester to support them through pregnancy and postpartum labor. Coordination care will be offered through Piedmont hospital (Athens, Macon, and Walton). Participating women will receive initial prenatal care exam consisting of labs and prenatal medication before connecting with the mobile unit for continuous routine checkups throughout pregnancy. Through the continuous checkups staff at the mobile unit will send medical documents logs over to partner hospitals to provide telehealth services for participating women that are in need of this service. Through the 2 years women will have access to prenatal, postpartum, mental health and a wide range of social services.

Services Provided

Mother's Aid offers comprehensive maternal healthcare services at no cost to eligible participants. Our services cover all stages of pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum care.

Supporting You Every Step of the Way

Services for Newly Pregnant Mothers

Early Pregnancy Care
  • Dating ultrasound (if available)
  • Medical history review
  • Enrollment into prenatal care
Initial Health Screening
  • Blood pressure, weight, BMI
  • Blood tests (CBC, blood type-Rh, HIV, ophtho, hepatitis)
  • Urine tests (UT, glucose, protein etc.)
Education & Counseling
  • Nutrition guidance
  • Prenatal vitamins and folic acid
  • Lifestyle counseling
  • Warning signs to watch for

Social & Support Services

  • Referrals to OB/GYNists
  • Assistance with insurance /WIC
  • Mental health & substance use referrals
  • Housing support
  • Transportation coordination for clinic visits

Eligibility

Our services are available to:

  • Pregnant women residing in Hancock County
  • New mothers up to 1 year postpartum
  • All income levels welcome
  • Insured and uninsured patients
  • Women of all ages and backgrounds
